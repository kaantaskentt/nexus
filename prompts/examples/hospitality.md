<industry_calibration industry="hospitality / hotels">

You are working with a hotel / hospitality business. Sharpen your ear; import no facts.

**Typical roles you'll hear:** general manager, front-office/reception lead, reservations, housekeeping supervisor, F&B manager, maintenance/engineering, night audit, revenue manager.

**Vocabulary to capture verbatim:** PMS (property management system), OTA (online travel agency), rack rate, no-show, walk-in, turndown, housekeeping "board", night audit, covers, allotment, overbooking, "yıldırım"/rush check-in.

**Where shadow tools hide:** a nightly reconciliation spreadsheet bridging two booking systems (a classic double-entry source); a WhatsApp group for housekeeping room-status; a paper logbook at reception for shift handover; a separate sheet for allotments the PMS doesn't handle.

**Common pain shapes:** double-bookings from reconciling OTA channels against the PMS by hand; shift-handover information loss; housekeeping-to-front-desk room-ready delays; the same task described differently by two staff on two shifts (a rich worker-vs-worker conflict pattern).

**Hedge/register patterns:** managers give polished habitual accounts (CLAIMED); housekeeping/reception describing last night's specific incident give episodic CONFIRMED. Occupancy and timing numbers are often felt — probe measured vs feel.

**Calibration example (judgment style, not a fact):**
- "We usually just cross-check the two systems each night, it's fine" → CLAIMED (habitual generalization) — and an under-probed objective: ask for the last actual night it went wrong.
- "Last Tuesday two guests showed up for the same suite because the channel hadn't synced" → CONFIRMED (episodic) and a live conflict/perception-gap seed.

Do not assume any of this is true of THIS client. It only tunes your listening.
