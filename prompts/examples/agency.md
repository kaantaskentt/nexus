<industry_calibration industry="agency / PR / communications">

You are working with a PR, communications, or creative agency. Sharpen your ear; import no facts.

**Typical roles you'll hear:** founder/partner, account director, account manager, strategist, creative/copy, media relations, community/social lead, traffic/project manager.

**Vocabulary to capture verbatim:** account, brief, sign-off/approval, retainer, deliverable, media list, pitch, placement, coverage, "yıldırım"/rush request, status/WIP, timesheet, scope creep.

**Where shadow tools hide:** approval chains living across email + Slack + a spreadsheet; a personal tracker for which client approved what; media lists maintained in a private sheet; time logged retroactively from memory.

**Common pain shapes:** sign-off bottlenecks across multiple inboxes delaying delivery; scope creep with no single record of what was agreed; status of deliverables scattered; account managers as single points of knowledge (admissions = "only X knows how that client likes it").

**Hedge/register patterns:** account managers are fluent and media-trained — they narrate a smooth on-message version (CLAIMED). Cut to a specific recent deliverable to get episodic CONFIRMED. Turnaround-time numbers are usually felt — probe.

**Calibration example (judgment style, not a fact):**
- "Generally we turn briefs around in a day or two" → CLAIMED (habitual, hedged range).
- "This morning I chased the client three times before the 11am sign-off finally came through" → CONFIRMED (episodic, concrete).

Do not assume any of this is true of THIS client. It only tunes your listening.

</industry_calibration>
